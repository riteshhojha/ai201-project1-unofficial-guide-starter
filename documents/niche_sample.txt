# Niche Student Reviews

Overall Experience (4.2/5 stars): "Great school with strong academics but expensive. Financial aid varies widely. The campus is beautiful and safe. Community feel is strong if you get involved. Definitely worth visiting before applying - the tour doesn't capture the real vibe."

Academics (4.5/5 stars): "Professors genuinely care about teaching. Class sizes are reasonable even for introductory courses. Liberal arts requirement is annoying but actually makes you a well-rounded student. Workload is heavy but manageable if you start assignments early."

Campus Life (3.8/5 stars): "Lots of clubs and activities. Sports scene is competitive but welcoming to non-athletes. Greek life is present but not required to have fun. Weekend activities exist beyond parties if you look for them. Some weekends feel dead if you're not into the party scene."

Dorms (3.2/5 stars): "First year dorms are cramped but functional. You make friends fast though. Upper-level housing varies dramatically in quality. Some juniors are in nice apartments, others in terrible converted buildings. Lottery system is frustrating. Many students move off-campus to avoid housing issues."

Food (2.9/5 stars): "Dining hall quality is below average. Too many options means consistently mediocre execution. Salad bar is fresh but limited. Pizza and pasta are decent standby options. Freshman meal plan is expensive for the quality. Many students switch to smaller plans or eat off-campus after first year."

Social Scene (3.7/5 stars): "People here are friendly and welcoming. Possible to have non-alcoholic social life but requires effort. Various affinity groups and clubs create different communities. Diverse student body in numbers but social circles can be cliquey. International student integration could be better."

Value (3.4/5 stars): "Cost is high relative to some peer institutions. Financial aid packages are generous for some, disappointing for others. If you can negotiate, do it. Out-of-state cost is significant. Consider alternatives if cost is your main concern. Worth the investment if you're getting good support."

Safety & Environment (4.1/5 stars): "Campus is generally safe and well-maintained. Good lighting in most areas. Campus security is responsive. Some neighborhoods surrounding campus are less safe. Violent crime is rare but property crime happens. Feel comfortable walking around during day, cautious at night."
