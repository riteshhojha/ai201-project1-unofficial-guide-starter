# Sample College Reviews

## Professor Quality & Workload
Professor Miller is engaging and knows the material well, but expects a lot from students. Exams are fairly graded and she gives partial credit generously. Expect 3-4 hours of homework per week for CS major courses. Students say her office hours are invaluable if you're struggling.

The introductory biology professor has terrible delivery and reading comprehension is optional for his exams. Reviews on Rate My Professors consistently mention he tests on material never discussed in lecture. Most pre-med students recommend finding a tutor by week 2.

Chemistry professors here are known for harsh grading on problem sets, but the lab component is where you actually learn. Chemistry department doesn't curve exams, so studying with classmates is essential. Average student spends 10-15 hours per week on chemistry coursework.

## Campus Life & Social Environment
Dorm life first year is cramped but built community. Upper-level housing is significantly better if you get into the good dorms. Housing lottery system is frustrating and unfair. Many juniors and seniors move off-campus to avoid the lottery.

Dining hall food is consistently mediocre. Harris dining has the best selection but the most crowded. Breakfast is actually decent if you go before 9am. Many students get meal plans with fewer meals after freshman year and use the dining points strategically.

Social scene is cliquey if you don't join clubs early. Greek life dominates weekends but there are non-Greek alternatives. LGBTQ+ community is visible and supported but still navigating inclusion issues. Mental health resources exist but have long wait times for counseling.

## Financial & Post-Grad Outcomes
Financial aid packages vary wildly. Merit scholarships are competitive. Out-of-state students often pay significantly more. Student loans are required unless family income is very high. Many students work part-time jobs to cover living expenses.

Internship recruiting is strong in finance and tech, weaker in humanities. Career services office is helpful but understaffed. Class of 2023 graduates had mixed outcomes: tech jobs paid 70-90k starting, liberal arts majors took longer to find roles. Network is strong if you use it.

Post-grad employment data isn't transparent. Many recent grads took jobs outside their major. Graduate school acceptance rates are decent but no official statistics published. Alumni network is active in coastal cities, sparse elsewhere.

## Specific Complaints & Praise
The registrar's office is a nightmare for course conflicts. Registration is first-come-first-served and servers crash every year. Getting a straight answer about requirements takes visiting 3 different offices. Graduation requirements are confusing and poorly documented.

Library is beautiful and well-maintained. Study spaces are usually available except during midterms and finals. WiFi is reliable. Printing system is outdated and frustrating.

The administration is responsive to student concerns about housing and safety. Campus is well-lit and feels safe at night. Parking on campus is nearly impossible; most students use street parking or the overflow lot 1.5 miles away.
